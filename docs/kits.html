Router & Mesh Buying FAQ

Is Wi-Fi 7 worth it over Wi-Fi 6E?

Yes if you have multi-gig internet, many new 6E/7 devices, or congestion—otherwise 6E remains excellent for most homes.

Mesh or single router for ~3,000 sq ft?

Mesh (2–3 nodes) wins for multi-floor or brick/concrete homes; a single high-end router can work in open layouts.

Do I need 2.5/5/10GbE?

Use multi-gig WAN/LAN if your plan exceeds 1 Gbps, you transfer large files, or you run a NAS/pro-grade wired backbone.

Will these work with cable, fiber, 5G, or satellite?

Yes—select a WAN tier that matches your modem/ONT. All listed kits support cable/fiber; many also handle 5G/satellite.

How many devices can a kit handle?

Use the device filter. Typical Wi-Fi 7 mesh handles 100–200+ devices; mid-range Wi-Fi 6/6E handles ~30–100.
